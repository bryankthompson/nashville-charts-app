  AMAZING GRACE
  Traditional Hymn (Public Domain)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Key:     G major
  Tuning:  Standard
  Capo:    No capo
  Time:    3/4
  Tempo:   ~72 BPM
  Feel:    Slow waltz, reverent
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHORD MAP (Key of G)
  1 = G    4 = C    5 = D    5/7 = D/F#

SONG MAP
  Verse 1:   1 - 4 - 1 - 1 - 5 - 1 - 4 - 1 - 5 - 1
  Verse 2-4: (same chord pattern)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[VERSE 1]
1               4      1
  A - maz - ing grace, how sweet the sound
1              5
  That saved a wretch like me
1              4       1
  I once was lost but now am found
1    5         1
  Was blind but now I see

[VERSE 2]
1                4        1
  'Twas grace that taught my heart to fear
1                 5
  And grace my fears relieved
1                 4       1
  How precious did that grace appear
1    5            1
  The hour I first believed

[VERSE 3]
1                4         1
  Through many dangers, toils and snares
1                5
  I have already come
1                 4       1
  'Tis grace hath brought me safe thus far
1    5            1
  And grace will lead me home

[VERSE 4]
1               4         1
  When we've been there ten thousand years
1                 5
  Bright shining as the sun
1                4        1
  We've no less days to sing God's praise
1    5            1
  Than when we'd first begun

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
NOTES:
  - Classic 1-4-1-5-1 hymn structure in 3/4 waltz time
  - Words by John Newton (1772), public domain
  - Key of G is the most common congregational key
  - Contemporary arrangements (Chris Tomlin) often add a chorus section and modulate up
  - The 5/7 (D/F#) can be used as a passing chord between 1 and 4
